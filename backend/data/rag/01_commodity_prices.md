## Rice and staple grains

Wholesale rice prices often move with export demand, government buffer stock releases, fuel and milling costs, and monsoon-linked paddy availability. Import parity and currency stability also shift domestic quotes.

## Oil and transport

Diesel and shipping costs feed through to farmgate and wholesale food prices. Freight indices and port congestion can widen spreads between producing and consuming regions.

## Vegetables and perishables

Tomato, onion, and garlic prices are volatile around festivals, heat waves, and transport disruptions. Short harvest windows and cold-chain limits amplify spikes.

## Meat fish eggs

Poultry and egg prices react to feed costs (maize, soy), disease outbreaks, and seasonal demand. Beef and pork track import availability and slaughter capacity.

## Pulses and oilseeds

Bean and oilseed markets follow global oil and meal complexes, biodiesel policy, and regional harvest pressure.
