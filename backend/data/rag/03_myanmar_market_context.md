## Policy and trade

Export restrictions, minimum prices, and border trade rules can move domestic staple quotations quickly. Sanctions and banking frictions may widen bid-offer spreads.

## Exchange rate and inflation

Kyat volatility and broad inflation pass through to imported inputs (fuel, fertilizer) and to urban retail demand for staples.

## Logistics corridors

Truck availability, checkpoints, and river transport affect time-to-market for perishables. Conflict or security incidents can reroute flows and add costs.

## Survey and WFP-style prices

Retail and market-survey prices are often monthly snapshots; national medians smooth idiosyncratic market noise but lag fast-moving shocks.
